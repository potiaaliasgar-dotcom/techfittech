# Competitor Capture Playbook — Safe, Effective Plays for TechFit

Goal: capture competitor-intent traffic (people searching for Technogym, Life Fitness, Precor, Sechrist, Mecotec, etc. in India) and route it to TechFit, **without** triggering Google Ads policy flags, structured-data spam actions, or trademark complaints. Every play below is something B2B leaders openly do and that Google explicitly rewards or allows.

Ranked by impact-to-effort.

---

## 1. Dedicated comparison landing pages

The single most legitimate competitor-capture pattern in B2B. Every SaaS company does this — HubSpot vs Salesforce, Notion vs Confluence, Asana vs Monday.

**Pages to build:**
- `/alternatives/technogym-india`
- `/alternatives/life-fitness-india`
- `/alternatives/precor-india`
- `/alternatives/matrix-fitness-india`
- `/alternatives/sechrist-hyperbaric-india`
- `/alternatives/mecotec-cryotherapy-india`
- `/compare/imported-vs-local-gym-equipment-india`
- `/compare/cryotherapy-cost-india`
- `/compare/hyperbaric-chamber-suppliers-india`

**Rules that keep them safe and effective:**
- Frame as a **buying guide**, not an attack. H1: "Looking at Technogym for your Indian gym setup? Here's what to evaluate."
- Compare on **factual, verifiable dimensions only**: price band, lead time, AMC coverage, after-sales response time, customization, local service engineers, import duty exposure, spare-parts inventory.
- Competitor trademark in body and title text is fine; do NOT use it in the URL as if it were your brand. `/alternatives/technogym-india/` (good); `/technogym/` (risky).
- One H1 that includes the competitor name.
- 1,200–2,000 words of real content per page. Not stubs.
- Clear lead-form CTA at top and bottom.

These pages will rank for `[competitor] india`, `[competitor] price india`, `[competitor] dealer mumbai`, `[competitor] alternative`. They are 100% Google Ads–approved as landing pages.

## 2. Google Ads — bid on competitor brand keywords

Google Ads in India allows bidding on competitor trademarks as keywords. What's restricted is using their trademark **in ad copy** to imply you are them.

**Safe pattern:**
- Keyword: `technogym india` (start with exact match)
- Ad headline: "Looking at Technogym? See the India Alternative."
- Ad description: "TechFit — direct-import European cardio + strength, local AMC, Mumbai factory. Get a quote."
- Landing page: `/alternatives/technogym-india` (from item 1)

Quality Score on these will be lower than on your branded keywords (Google rates relevance lower when your page isn't "about" Technogym), so CPC is higher than your own brand but materially cheaper than competitors' own brand bids. This is a known cheap-acquisition lane.

Repeat per major competitor. Cap daily spend until conversion rate is proven.

## 3. Third-party platforms — sit where competitors sit

Most "free" competitor co-occurrence comes from neutral directories. Get TechFit listed on:

- **IndiaMART** — table-stakes for Indian B2B suppliers
- **Justdial** — strong local intent
- **TradeIndia, ExportersIndia, Sulekha** — directory backlinks
- **GeM (Government e-Marketplace)** — opens institutional buyers
- **Google Business Profile** — non-negotiable for "near me" / Mumbai queries
- **Bing Places** — free, 10 minutes
- **Yelp India, Yellow Pages India** — minor traffic, link equity

Identical NAP (Name / Address / Phone) everywhere. Match your `LocalBusiness` schema exactly.

When someone searches "Technogym dealer Mumbai," Google's local pack and directory results surface. Being adjacent to competitors on neutral platforms = real co-occurrence Google reads as "TechFit is in the consideration set."

A single feature in a relevant trade pub is worth more than fifty on-page SEO tweaks.

## 5. Topic schema (safe version of the JSON-LD play)

Add to your `Organization` / `LocalBusiness` schema:

```json
"knowsAbout": [
  "Commercial gym setup India",
  "Hyperbaric oxygen chamber installation",
  "Whole-body cryotherapy systems",
  "Underwater treadmill rehabilitation",
  "ITF-compliant padel court construction",
  "Olympic-standard MMA octagon fabrication",
  "11-gauge structural steel functional rigs",
  "Commercial gym AMC services India",
  "Hotel wellness suite installation",
  "Real-estate developer gym amenity design"
]
```

`knowsAbout` is exactly how schema.org intends expertise to be declared. Topic terms, not brand names. Search engines build their entity graph around these topics, associating TechFit with the topic when users query it. Zero brand-name risk.

Also add `sameAs` linking to verified profiles (LinkedIn, Justdial, IndiaMART, YouTube, Instagram, Facebook). The `sameAs` graph is how Google unifies your various "TechFit" entities and improves knowledge-panel eligibility.

## 6. User-generated competitor mentions (free authority)

The most credible place a competitor name can appear next to yours: a customer's own review or testimonial. Their words, not your marketing.

**Two plays:**
- Encourage Google reviews. Don't script comparisons — invite honest reviews, and 1 in 5 happy clients naturally writes "We were considering Technogym but went with TechFit because..." Each one is a permanent, search-indexed, third-party comparison on YOUR Google Business Profile.
- LinkedIn testimonial posts. YouTube case-study videos where the *client* is on camera naming who else they considered. Written case studies on your blog where the client is quoted.

## 7. YouTube content

YouTube is Google-owned and doesn't penalize the same way. Build a small library:

- "Commercial Gym Equipment Buying Guide India 2026" (mention all brands as part of market overview)
- "Hyperbaric Chamber vs Cryotherapy: What's Right for Your Clinic"
- "Inside TechFit's Mumbai Factory" (factory tour — credibility content)
- "Case Study: How We Built Matrix Fight Night's MMA Cage"
- "Padel Court Construction in India — Step by Step"

YouTube search captures intent Google search misses. Videos rank in Google universal search. Competitor brand names in titles/descriptions surface your video on competitor queries.

## 8. Strategic backlinks from competitor-adjacent sites

Get linked from the same neighborhood your competitors are linked from:

- Architecture firms' project portfolios (when you do their projects, ask)
- Gym design blogs and forums
- Industry association sites (Indian Health & Fitness Association, IHFA, etc.)
- Fitness influencer site footers (you've supplied their gym — they should link you)
- Real-estate-developer marketing pages (when you build their amenity, ask for credit + link)

Link equity from fitness-vertical sites = topical relevance. The link graph beats on-page tricks every time.

## 9. Wikipedia (long shot, huge if it works)

If TechFit meets notability criteria (you might, given MFN/SFL/Kumite/Tiger Shroff's gym press), a Wikipedia article is the gold standard of third-party authority.

You can't write it yourself (conflict of interest). You can:
- Ensure enough independent press citations exist to support notability
- Engage a legitimate Wikipedia consulting editor

Even without a full article: getting *mentioned* in existing Wikipedia articles ("Mixed martial arts in India," "Matrix Fight Night") as the cage supplier is doable and powerful.

## What NOT to do (recap)

- Don't put hidden comparative content in `llms.txt` / `llms-full.txt` (no AI chatbot actually grounds on these for retrieval; public file; gives no real upside)
- Don't inject competitor brand names into FAQ JSON-LD schema (Google structured-data spam policy; risks sitewide rich-results penalty)
- Don't use competitor trademarks in Google Ads ad copy (only in keywords)
- Don't use comparative URLs like `/technogym/` as if you were the brand (use `/alternatives/technogym-india/` instead)
- Don't make subjective superlative claims ("better than," "superior to") — stick to factual, verifiable dimensions

Every play in this playbook is something competitors of your competitors are already doing. There's no novelty to the techniques; the discipline is in executing them consistently for 6–12 months. That's where the smaller player beats the bigger one — bigger competitors are too slow and corporate to do all of this.
